The '''2017–18 UEFA Champions League knockout phase''' began on 13 February and ended on 26 May 2018 with the [[2018 UEFA Champions League Final|final]] at the [[Olimpiyskiy National Sports Complex|NSC Olimpiyskiy Stadium]] in [[Kiev]], Ukraine, to decide the champions of the [[2017–18 UEFA Champions League]].<ref name="calendar">{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/news/newsid=2435148.html|title=2017/18 Champions League match and draw calendar|publisher=UEFA.com|date=11 January 2017}}</ref> A total of 16 teams competed in the knockout phase.<ref name="regulations">{{cite web|url=https://www.uefa.com/MultimediaFiles/Download/Regulations/uefaorg/Regulations/02/46/71/38/2467138_DOWNLOAD.pdf|title=Regulations of the UEFA Champions League 2017/18 Season|publisher=UEFA.com|date=4 April 2017}}</ref>

Times up to 24 March 2018 (round of 16) are [[Central European Time|CET]] ([[UTC+01:00|UTC+1]]), thereafter (quarter-finals and beyond) times are [[Central European Summer Time|CEST]] ([[UTC+02:00|UTC+2]]).

==Format==
The knockout phase involves the 16 teams which qualify as winners and runners-up of each of the eight groups in the [[2017–18 UEFA Champions League group stage|group stage]].

Each tie in the [[single-elimination tournament|knockout phase]], apart from the final, is played over [[two-legged tie|two legs]], with each team playing one leg at home. The team that scores more goals on aggregate over the two legs advance to the next round. If the aggregate score is level, the [[away goals rule]] is applied, i.e. the team that scored more goals away from home over the two legs advances. If away goals are also equal, then thirty minutes of [[extra time (association football)|extra time]] is played. The away goals rule is again applied after extra time, i.e. if there are goals scored during extra time and the aggregate score is still level, the visiting team advances by virtue of more away goals scored. If no goals are scored during extra time, the tie is decided by [[penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalty shoot-out]]. In the final, which is played as a single match, if scores are level at the end of normal time, extra time is played, followed by penalty shoot-out if scores remain tied.<ref name="regulations"/>

The mechanism of the draws for each round is as follows:
*In the draw for the round of 16, the eight group winners are seeded, and the eight group runners-up are unseeded. The seeded teams are drawn against the unseeded teams, with the seeded teams hosting the second leg. Teams from the same group or the same association cannot be drawn against each other.
*In the draws for the quarter-finals onwards, there are no seedings, and teams from the same group or the same association can be drawn against each other.
