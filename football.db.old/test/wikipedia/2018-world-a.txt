===Russia vs Saudi Arabia===
The two teams had met only once, a friendly game in 1993 won by Saudi Arabia 4–2.<ref name=Kit3>{{cite web|url=https://resources.fifa.com/image/upload/2018-fifa-world-cup-russiatm-statistical-kit.pdf?cloudid=fjqkfby8aur1lxnvnxdi|title=2018 FIFA World Cup – Statistical Kit|publisher=FIFA.com|page=3}}</ref>

In the 12th minute, [[Yury Gazinsky]] headed the first goal for Russia from a cross from the left before substitute [[Denis Cheryshev]] evaded several challenges and smashed home at the near post close to half-time. The tempo slowed and the atmosphere flattened after the restart, giving the whole thing the feel of a friendly match. But substitute [[Artem Dzyuba]] illuminated a match of often dubious quality with a precise header from a cross from the right to extend his team's lead just minutes after replacing [[Fyodor Smolov]].<ref>{{citeweb|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2018/jun/14/russia-saudi-arabia-world-cup-2018-match-report|title = Russia coast past Saudi Arabia and leave them all smiling in Putin’s place|date=14 June 2018|work=Guardian|accessdate=15 June 2018}}</ref> As the match moved into injury time, Cheryshev smashed home with a crisp left-foot strike into the top right corner before [[Aleksandr Golovin (footballer)|Aleksandr Golovin]] curled a free-kick around the wall and into the right of the net.<ref name="BBC 5-0">{{cite web|last1=Fletcher |first1=Paul |title=Russia 5 Saudi Arabia 0 |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/44392911 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=14 June 2018}}</ref>

[[Alan Dzagoev]] limped off with a hamstring injury in the 24th minute. "Alan Dzagoev damaged the muscles in the back of the thigh," Russia said in a brief statement.<ref>{{cite web|title=Blow for Russia as Dzagoev limps off with hamstring injury in World Cup opener |url=http://www.goal.com/en-in/news/blow-for-russia-as-dzagoev-limps-off-with-hamstring-injury/1rdfvov69h8171tkwfzhxbx36b |work=Goal.com |date=14 June 2018}}</ref>

For the first time in World Cup history, two players – Gazinsky and Cheryshev – scored their first international goals in the opening match.<ref>{{cite web |title=Hosts Russia kick off World Cup in style |url=https://www.fifa.com/worldcup/news/hosts-russia-kick-off-world-cup-in-style |publisher=FIFA.com |date=14 June 2018}}</ref> Russia's victory means that the host nation has never lost their opening match in any of the 21 editions of the World Cup (won 16, drew six). Russia (5–0) recorded the second biggest victory by a host nation in their opening match at a World Cup, after Italy v USA in 1934 (7–1); while it is also the second largest victory of any host nation, only behind Italy vs USA at 1934 and Brazil vs Sweden at 1950, both ended 7–1. Russia's Denis Cheryshev became the first substitute to score a goal in the opening match of a World Cup tournament. In this game, Sergei Ignashevich became the oldest player to ever appear in a World Cup match for Russia/USSR (38 years & 335 days old).<ref name="BBC 5-0"/><ref>{{cite web |last1=Staunton |first1=Peter |title=Golovin genius caps dream World Cup start for 'worst Russia' ever |url=http://www.goal.com/en-ie/news/golovin-genius-caps-dream-world-cup-start-for-worst-russia/1g7ibqj81p1r718esh12hh3j4e |publisher=Goal.com |date=14 June 2018}}</ref>

For Saudi Arabia, this was their second biggest defeat in World Cup, after the [[2002 FIFA World Cup Group E#Germany vs Saudi Arabia|0–8]] defeat to Germany in [[2002 FIFA World Cup|2002]].

===Egypt vs Uruguay===
The two teams had met only once, a friendly game in 2006 won by Uruguay 2–0.<ref name=Kit3/>

Egypt frustrated Uruguay with a deep defensive line, staying compact, dealing with danger competently and threatening on the counter-attack to maintain the scoreline goalless, nearing the end of the match.<ref>{{cite news |title=Jose Gimenez grabs late winner for Uruguay to break Egyptian hearts |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/world-cup/2018/06/15/world-cup-2018-egypt-vs-uruguay-live-score-latest-updates/ |work=The Telegraph |date=15 June 2018}}</ref> [[Luis Suárez]] looked increasingly at a loss but remained prominent in the few opportunities that were created. When he had just the goalkeeper to beat, he dithered too long and allowed [[Mohamed El-Shenawy]] to muffle the ball at his feet. With three minutes remaining, [[Edinson Cavani]] pulled rank over his strike partner over a free kick on the edge of the area. Having earlier exerted El-Shenawy to the maximum with a fine volley, Cavani left the post shaking with what was undoubtedly the game’s best moment to that point.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Bairner |first1=Robin |title=No Salah, no party as Suarez sinks on World Cup return |url=http://www.goal.com/en/news/no-salah-no-party-as-suarez-sinks-on-world-cup-return/1y1h3oiyni4r0152nqtepn8umn |publisher=Goal.com |date=15 June 2018}}</ref> [[José Giménez]] headed powerfully into the net in the 89th minute as he met [[Carlos Andrés Sánchez|Carlos Sánchez]]'s free-kick from the right to give the South Americans a winning start in Russia.<ref name="BBC 0-1">{{cite web |last1=Emons |first1=Michael |title=Egypt 0 Uruguay 1 |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/44408321 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=15 June 2018}}</ref>

Uruguay won their opening match at the World Cup tournament for the first time since [[1970 FIFA World Cup|1970]], when they beat Israel [[1970 FIFA World Cup Group 2#Uruguay vs Israel|2–0]].<ref name="BBC 0-1"/><ref>{{cite web |title=Pain for returning Egypt as Uruguay earn late win |url=https://www.fifa.com/worldcup/news/pain-for-returning-egypt-as-uruguay-earn-late-win |publisher=FIFA.com |date=15 June 2018}}</ref>

===Uruguay vs Russia===
The two teams have met in one previous game, a friendly in 2012, which ended in a 1–1 draw. Playing as the [[Soviet Union national football team]], the two teams faced each other seven times, including two matches at the World Cup, one in the [[1962 FIFA World Cup]] group stage, won by the Soviet Union [[1962 FIFA World Cup Group 1#Soviet Union vs Uruguay|2–1]], and the other one at the [[1970 FIFA World Cup]] quarter-finals, won by Uruguay [[1970 FIFA World Cup knockout stage#Soviet Union vs Uruguay|1–0]].<ref name=Kit4/>
