===Morocco vs Iran===
The two teams had never met before.<ref name=Kit>{{cite web|url=https://resources.fifa.com/image/upload/2018-fifa-world-cup-russiatm-statistical-kit.pdf?cloudid=fjqkfby8aur1lxnvnxdi |title=2018 FIFA World Cup – Statistical Kit|publisher=FIFA.com}}</ref>

Morocco, competing at the World Cup finals for the first time in 20 years, started positively and [[Amine Harit]] fired wide with a volley from the edge of the penalty area after two minutes. The pressure on Iran was almost relentless, with [[Younès Belhanda]] and captain [[Medhi Benatia]] squandering close-range chances in a penalty box scramble before Iran finally countered after 20 minutes, only for [[Karim Ansarifard]] to scuff his shot when clean through on goal. The best chance of the first half fell to Iran's [[Sardar Azmoun]], though, who bore down on goal after latching onto [[Omid Ebrahimi]]'s through ball, but his shot was too low and [[Munir Mohand Mohamedi|Munir Mohamedi]] saved low with his feet.<ref name="Goal-Morocco v Iran">{{cite web |last1=Creek |first1=Stephen |title=Bouhaddouz own-goal gifts game to Queiroz's men |url=http://www.goal.com/en/news/8/main/2018/06/15/45550852/morocco-0-iran-1-bouhaddouz-own-goal-gifts-game-to-queirozs |publisher=Goal.com |date=15 June 2018}}</ref> Morocco pushed for a winner in the final 10 minutes and [[Hakim Ziyech]] lashed the ball towards the bottom-left corner of the net from 20 yards but [[Alireza Beiranvand]] pulled off a smart stop. And it proved an even more important save when Iran forced a free-kick wide on the left in the closing seconds and [[Ehsan Hajsafi]]'s delivery was met by a thunderbolt of a header from [[Aziz Bouhaddouz]] that flew into the left corner of the net, undoing Morocco's hard work and leaving them with a mountain to climb to stay in the World Cup.<ref name="Goal-Morocco v Iran"/><ref name="BBC 0-1">{{cite web |last1=Emons |first1=Michael |title=Morocco 0 Iran 1 |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/44408322 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=15 June 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Own goal hands Iran dramatic victory |url=https://www.fifa.com/worldcup/news/iran-snatch-late-win-with-own-goal-heartbreak |publisher=FIFA.com |date=15 June 2018}}</ref>

With just under 20 minutes left on the clock, [[Nordin Amrabat]] fell awkwardly when challenging for the ball and the wing-back was left so dazed that he had to be replaced by his younger brother, [[Sofyan Amrabat|Sofyan]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Gordon |first1=Jamie |title=Medical team cause a stir with unusual attempt to help Nordin Amrabat with apparent concussion |url=https://www.thesun.co.uk/world-cup-2018/6543904/morocco-vs-iran-nordin-amrabat-concussion/ |publisher=The Sun |date=15 June 2018}}</ref>

Iran won just their second match at the World Cup (D3 L8), with this their first since beating USA [[1998 FIFA World Cup Group F#United States vs Iran|2–1]] in [[1998 FIFA World Cup|June 1998]]. Iran did not attempt a single shot in the second half and were the first team since [[1966 FIFA World Cup|1966]] to score a goal in a half of World Cup football without attempting a shot.<ref name="Goal-Morocco v Iran"/><ref name="BBC 0-1"/>

===Portugal vs Spain===
The two teams had met in 35 previous matches, including one semi-quarter in the [[2010 FIFA World Cup|2010 World Cup]], won by Spain [[2010 FIFA World Cup knockout stage#Spain vs Portugal|1–0]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://resources.fifa.com/image/upload/2018-fifa-world-cup-russiatm-statistical-kit.pdf?cloudid=fjqkfby8aur1lxnvnxdi|title=2018 FIFA World Cup – Statistical Kit|publisher=FIFA.com|page=6|format=PDF}}</ref>

[[Cristiano Ronaldo]] kicked off with an early penalty, deceiving [[Nacho (footballer, born 1990)|Nacho]] inside the box to win it and then stroking the ball home from the spot.<ref name="FIFA Portugal v Spain">{{cite web |title=Ronaldo illuminates stellar draw with Spain |url=https://www.fifa.com/worldcup/news/156-por-esp-match-report-x-x-2957787 |publisher=FIFA.com |date=15 June 2018}}</ref> [[Diego Costa]] then bullied [[Pepe (footballer, born 1983)|Pepe]] in the air, then twisted and turned away from two Portugal defenders to rifle home the goal which brought the two sides level.<ref name="Goal Portugal v Spain">{{cite web |last1=Smith |first1=Jamie |title=Brilliant Ronaldo hat-trick earns thrilling draw |url=http://www.goal.com/en-in/match/portugal-v-spain/ca5gvudcr66pasfhzalrq0bmx |publisher=Goal.com |date=15 June 2018}}</ref> But just before the break [[David de Gea]] made a terrible and uncharacteristic error, fumbling a zipping Ronaldo shot through his gloves and over the line.<ref name="BBC Portugal v Spain">{{cite web |last1=Jennings |first1=Patrick |title=Portugal 3 Spain 3 |url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/football/44408323 |publisher=BBC Sport |date=15 June 2018}}</ref> Costa scored his second equaliser of the night just after the restart before Nacho atoned for his penalty-box trip on Ronaldo by powering a superb half-volley in off the post, his first goal for Spain. As the clock ticked down, however, [[Gerard Piqué]] brought down Ronaldo just outside the box, with the Portugal star dusting himself down to complete the scoring with a sublime free-kick.<ref name="FIFA Portugal v Spain"/>

Ronaldo became the fourth player to score in four different World Cup tournaments ([[2006 FIFA World Cup|2006]], [[2010 FIFA World Cup|2010]], [[2014 FIFA World Cup|2014]] and [[2018 FIFA World Cup|2018]]) after [[Pelé]], [[Uwe Seeler]] and [[Miroslav Klose]], and also the first to score in eight consecutive World Cups and [[UEFA European Championship|European Championships]].{{refn|group=note|[[Ghana national football team|Ghana]]'s [[Asamoah Gyan]] scored in nine consecutive World Cups and [[Africa Cup of Nations]] tournaments between 2006 and 2017.<ref>{{cite web |title=Cristiano Ronaldo stat is false, I have tournament record - Asamoah Gyan |url=http://global.espn.com/football/fifa-world-cup/story/3528206/cristiano-ronaldo-record-is-stat-i-have-tournament-record-asamoah-gyan |publisher=ESPN}}</ref>}} At age 33, Ronaldo also became the oldest hat-trick scorer in the World Cup, surpassing [[Rob Rensenbrink]] of the [[Netherlands national football team|Netherlands]], who [[1978 FIFA World Cup Group 4#Netherlands vs Iran|scored three times against Iran]] in [[1978 FIFA World Cup|1978]], aged 30.<ref name="Goal Portugal v Spain"/><ref name="BBC Portugal v Spain"/> With this result, Spain has not won any opening matches since 2010, having lost to Switzerland 0–1 in 2010 and the Netherlands 1–5 in 2014.
